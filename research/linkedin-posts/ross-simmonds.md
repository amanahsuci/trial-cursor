## Post 1 — April 2026

I built Distribution.ai because the creation problem was mostly solved.

AI can write. AI can design. AI can produce at scale. What it cannot do without 
strategy is get that content in front of the right person at the right moment 
on the right channel.

Distribution is still the hard part. And it always will be.

**Topic:** Why distribution — not creation — is the real moat in content marketing.

## Post 2 — April 2026

The most indispensable person on your marketing team probably doesn't have 
"engineer" in their title. They're the one who built the automated content audit, 
connected the CRM to reporting, managed 500 product pages without hiring three 
more people.

The model: hire or develop one builder who creates leverage across all channels — 
competitive intelligence, sales enablement, PR monitoring, review generation — 
instead of hiring for every new channel.

Strategy stays human. Systems scale the rest.

**Topic:** The rise of the Marketing Engineer — one systems-thinking hire creates 
more leverage than multiple channel-specific hires.

## Post 3 — April 2026

Your website is not your best salesperson. Your customers are.

73% of B2B decision-makers say peer recommendations are their most trusted 
information channel — beating homepages, case studies, and SEO content.

The smarter move: obsess over the post-sale experience. Turn customers into content. 
Make it easy for them to talk about you in the places buyers are already looking. 
Word of mouth doesn't just happen. It gets engineered.

**Topic:** Customer-led growth — peer recommendations outperform all owned content 
channels in B2B. Engineer word of mouth, don't wait for it.
